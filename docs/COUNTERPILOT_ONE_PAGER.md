# Counterpilot One-Pager

## Positioning

Counterpilot is a Shopify make-an-offer app that tracks negotiated orders
through payment, refunds, returns, and maturity so merchants can see true mature
margin, not just accepted offers.

## Who It Is For

Counterpilot is for Shopify merchants who:

- Sell products where negotiation or discount requests are common.
- Care about contribution margin after costs, refunds, and returns.
- Want to approve every offer decision manually.
- Need a simple private-beta workflow before trusting automation.

It is not for stores that only need generic discount codes, fully automated
pricing, or broad analytics dashboards.

## Problem

Accepted offers are not the same as profitable orders. A negotiated order can
look good at checkout and still become weak after shipping cost, fees, refunds,
returns, or delayed maturity.

Most lightweight offer flows stop at acceptance. Counterpilot follows the order
until the merchant can see observed mature margin.

## How It Works

1. The merchant enables Make an Offer on selected products.
2. A shopper submits an offer.
3. The merchant manually accepts, counters, or declines.
4. If the buyer accepts, Counterpilot creates the Shopify checkout path.
5. Counterpilot ingests paid, refund, and return lifecycle events.
6. After the maturity window, Counterpilot writes a mature-margin report.

## Why It Is Different

Counterpilot is not trying to be a generic pricing tool. The private-beta wedge
is narrower:

- Manual-first offer handling.
- Shopify-native checkout creation.
- Refund and return aware lifecycle tracking.
- Mature-margin reporting instead of accepted-offer counts.
- Explicit non-causal reporting language.

## What It Does Not Claim

Counterpilot does not claim:

- Conversion lift.
- Profit lift.
- Recovered revenue.
- AI pricing.
- Automated negotiation.
- What would have happened without Counterpilot.

Merchant accept, counter, and decline decisions are manual in Private Beta v0.

## Private Beta Call To Action

Private beta is for a small number of merchants willing to test a manual-first
make-an-offer flow and review a mature-margin report after the lifecycle closes.

The first conversation should answer:

```text
Would this make negotiated orders easier to trust and review in your store?
```
